# LOUP — Brand Brief

## 1. Brand Overview

LOUP is a Lisbon-first fashion rental platform that gives people access to high-quality garments without needing to buy every piece they wear. It connects renters, wardrobe owners, independent designers, and curated local shops through a controlled, trust-based rental experience.

The brand is built around access, care, sustainability, and local community. LOUP is not about fast consumption or endless marketplace clutter. It is about helping people dress well for real moments while extending the life of existing garments.

## 2. One-Sentence Description

LOUP is a Lisbon-based fashion rental platform where people can access carefully selected, high-quality garments from local wardrobes, designers, and shops in a more sustainable way.

## 3. Brand Purpose

LOUP exists to make fashion feel more flexible, responsible, and accessible. Instead of encouraging people to constantly buy new clothes for occasional use, LOUP helps them rent pieces for events, travel, work, celebrations, and everyday moments where they want something special without long-term ownership.

The goal is to reduce waste, increase garment reuse, support local fashion ecosystems, and create a more intentional way of dressing.

## 4. Core Positioning

LOUP sits between fashion discovery, circular fashion, and local community access. It should feel curated, trustworthy, modern, and calm.

LOUP is not a discount rental marketplace. It is not a chaotic resale platform. It is not a fast-fashion alternative with lower prices as the main appeal. The value comes from quality, curation, local availability, sustainability, and trust.

## 5. Brand Values

### Quality

Every item on LOUP should feel worth renting. The platform prioritizes well-made garments, strong photography, clear fit information, and careful presentation. Quality applies not only to the clothes, but also to the interface, communication, and booking experience.

### Sustainability

LOUP promotes circular fashion by helping garments be worn more often and kept in use for longer. The brand should communicate sustainability in a grounded way, without sounding preachy or vague. The message is simple: better use of what already exists.

### Local Connection

LOUP begins in Lisbon and should feel connected to the city: its events, creative communities, independent fashion scene, and social rhythm. The local focus makes the service more personal, practical, and trustworthy.

### Trust

Because users are renting and lending personal items, trust is central. LOUP should feel safe, clear, and well-managed. Verification, item standards, rental status tracking, communication flows, and early manual support are part of the brand experience.

### Curation

The experience should feel selected rather than crowded. Users should not feel overwhelmed by endless listings. LOUP should guide people toward pieces that feel relevant, wearable, and appropriate for their needs.

### Ease

Renting should feel simple. The platform should reduce friction around discovery, booking, communication, handoff, and return. The tone should be clear and helpful, never complicated.

## 6. Target Audience

### Renters

LOUP is mainly designed for style-conscious people in Lisbon who want access to varied, high-quality garments without buying every piece. Early renters are likely to be women aged around 20–35 who attend events, value self-expression, care about sustainability, and want more outfit options without overconsumption.

They may need clothing for weddings, dinners, work events, parties, travel, photoshoots, or special weekends. They want variety, but they do not want the guilt, cost, or storage problem of constantly buying new items.

### Lenders

LOUP also serves people, designers, and shops with strong wardrobes or selected pieces that are underused. Lenders may want to earn from garments they already own, reach new customers, or participate in a more circular fashion model.

For lenders, the platform should feel low-effort, secure, and worth participating in. Support with listing quality, verification, item presentation, and rental management is important.

## 7. Brand Personality

LOUP should feel calm, refined, warm, and confident. It should have taste, but it should not feel distant or intimidating. The brand should invite people into a better way of accessing fashion without sounding elitist.

The personality should be:

- Thoughtful
- Modern
- Selective
- Warm
- Responsible
- Local
- Tasteful
- Clear

Avoid sounding loud, overly trendy, corporate, childish, or aggressively sales-driven.

## 8. Tone of Voice

LOUP’s voice should be simple, elegant, and direct. It should explain the idea clearly while making the experience feel desirable.

Use language that emphasizes:

- Access over ownership
- Quality over quantity
- Reuse over waste
- Local wardrobes over anonymous inventory
- Careful selection over endless browsing
- Confidence over hype

### Example Tone

Instead of: “Rent luxury fashion instantly.”  
Use: “Access high-quality pieces from Lisbon wardrobes, designers, and curated shops.”

Instead of: “The ultimate glamorous closet.”  
Use: “A better way to dress for the moments that matter.”

Instead of: “Cheap designer rentals.”  
Use: “Wear more, own less, and give great pieces more life.”

## 9. Key Messages

### Main Message

Access a curated local wardrobe without buying more than you need.

### Supporting Messages

- Rent high-quality garments for events, occasions, and everyday moments.
- Discover pieces from Lisbon wardrobes, independent designers, and selected shops.
- Give garments more life through a more circular fashion system.
- Dress with more variety while reducing unnecessary consumption.
- Lend underused pieces and turn your wardrobe into a source of value.
- A calmer, more intentional way to explore fashion.

## 10. Visual Direction

The visual identity should feel clean, tactile, editorial, and grounded. The design should have generous spacing, strong photography, and a controlled color palette.

### Design Principles

- Use whitespace generously.
- Let garment photography carry emotional value.
- Keep layouts calm and uncluttered.
- Use soft, natural textures when relevant.
- Use rounded or organic shapes subtly.
- Avoid visual noise, heavy gradients, and generic startup patterns.
- Prioritize clarity, polish, and trust.

## 11. Color System

### Core Colors

- Deep forest green: `#0F5E4B`
- Soft off-white: `#F9FAF7`
- Muted sage: `#B8C7B1`
- Warm stone: `#E7E4DF`

### Usage

Deep forest green should act as the main anchor color for the logo, primary actions, and selected highlights. Soft off-white should dominate backgrounds. Sage and warm stone should support secondary surfaces, cards, filters, dividers, and calm visual accents.

Avoid bright neon greens, harsh blacks, saturated gradients, or colors that make the product feel like a generic tech marketplace.

## 12. Typography Direction

Typography should feel modern, clear, and carefully spaced.

- Use Syne or a similar expressive sans-serif for headlines and brand moments.
- Use a clean, highly readable sans-serif for body text and interface copy.
- Keep headings spacious and confident.
- Avoid overly decorative fonts that reduce readability.
- Use hierarchy through size, spacing, and weight rather than excessive color changes.

## 13. Product Experience Principles

### Curated Browse

The browse experience should not feel like scrolling through random listings. It should feel selected, visual, and easy to filter by occasion, size, category, and availability.

### Clear Item Detail

Each item page should make quality and trust visible through strong photos, fit notes, size information, condition details, availability, lender profile, and rental terms.

### Trust-Based Booking

The booking flow should make the user feel guided. Dates, price, approval status, handoff or shipping steps, and return expectations should always be clear.

### Support in the Early Stage

Since LOUP is starting locally, early manual support is part of the experience. This can help with onboarding, quality checks, lender verification, and resolving edge cases.

## 14. Community Role

LOUP should grow as a local fashion network, not just a transaction platform. The community includes renters, lenders, independent designers, curated shops, stylists, and sustainability-conscious fashion users.

The platform should make people feel they are participating in a better fashion habit: sharing access, reducing waste, supporting local style, and giving existing garments more use.

## 15. What LOUP Should Avoid

- Looking like a generic marketplace.
- Presenting sustainability with vague claims.
- Overcrowding the interface with too many products at once.
- Using overly glamorous or exclusive language.
- Competing only on low price.
- Making the experience feel informal or risky.
- Treating lenders as secondary users.
- Using loud trend-based visuals that will age quickly.

## 16. Brand Tagline Options

- Your local wardrobe, shared with care.
- Wear more. Own less. Waste less.
- High-quality fashion, rented locally.
- A better way to dress in Lisbon.
- Access more style without buying more clothes.
- Give great garments more life.

## 17. Short Brand Description

LOUP is a Lisbon-first fashion rental platform for accessing high-quality garments from local wardrobes, independent designers, and curated shops. It helps people dress for events and everyday moments with more variety, less waste, and a stronger connection to the local fashion community.

## 18. Practical Brand Rule

Every LOUP touchpoint should answer three questions clearly:

1. Does this feel high-quality and trustworthy?
2. Does this support a more sustainable way of using fashion?
3. Does this feel connected to Lisbon and its local fashion community?
